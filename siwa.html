About Siwa

Siwa Oasis is one of Egypt's most isolated settlements, located near the Libyan border. This remote paradise is home to the Siwans, who maintain their own unique culture, traditions, and language called Siwi, a Berber dialect distinct from Arabic. What makes Siwa extraordinary is its unchanged way of life. The oasis is renowned for its exceptional dates and olives, natural springs, salt lakes, and mud-brick architecture that seamlessly blends with the landscape. Historically significant as the site of the Oracle of Amun, which Alexander the Great consulted in 331 BCE, Siwa today offers travelers a glimpse into a way of life that has remained largely unchanged for centuries, alongside breathtaking natural beauty.
