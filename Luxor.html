About Luxor

Luxor stands on the site of ancient Thebes, the pharaohs' capital during the height of their power during the 16th-11th centuries BC. The modern city has grown up around the stunning ancient monuments, creating a unique atmosphere where past and present coexist. The East Bank of the Nile is home to the city center, Luxor Temple, and the massive Karnak Temple complex. This was considered the "city of the living" in ancient times, where temples were built to celebrate life. The West Bank, considered the "city of the dead," contains the Valley of the Kings, Valley of the Queens, and numerous mortuary temples. Here, the ancient Egyptians built elaborate tombs and temples dedicated to the afterlife.

Best Time to Visit Luxor

Sinai can be visited year-round, but the best times are spring (March-May) and autumn (September
-November) when temperatures are moderate. Coastal areas remain pleasant even in summer, but the inland
desert can be extremely hot.
