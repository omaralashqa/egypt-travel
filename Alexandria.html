About Alexandria

Founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC, Alexandria was once one of the greatest cities in the world and home to the legendary Library of Alexandria and the Lighthouse — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Today, Alexandria blends its rich history with a cosmopolitan Mediterranean charm. The city features a unique mix of Greek, Roman, Egyptian, and European architectural influences, reflecting its diverse cultural heritage. With its beautiful corniche (waterfront promenade), delicious seafood, and relaxed atmosphere, Alexandria offers visitors a refreshing contrast to the desert landscapes found elsewhere in Egypt.

Best Time to Visit Alexandria

The ideal time to visit Alexandria is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November)
when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be humid due to the Mediterranean climate, while
winters are cool with occasional rainfall.
