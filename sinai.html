About Sinai Peninsula

The Sinai Peninsula is a triangular landmass bridging Africa and Asia, bounded by the Mediterranean Sea to the north, the Red Sea to the south, and the Suez Canal to the west. This region holds immense religious significance as the place where Moses received the Ten Commandments according to Jewish, Christian, and Islamic traditions. Its rugged mountains and deep valleys have served as a spiritual retreat for millennia. The coastal areas along the Red Sea have developed into popular resort destinations, with Sharm El-Sheikh and Dahab offering world-class diving and snorkeling. Meanwhile, the peninsula's interior remains home to Bedouin communities who maintain their traditional way of life.

Best Time to Visit Sinai

Sinai can be visited year-round, but the best times are spring (March-May) and autumn (September
-November) when temperatures are moderate. Coastal areas remain pleasant even in summer, but the inland
desert can be extremely hot.
